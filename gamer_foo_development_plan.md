## 3. Practice Schedule

### 3.1 Daily Practice Structure (90-120 minutes)

**Warmup Phase (15-20 minutes)**
- [ ] 5 min: Physical warmup (hand stretches, posture check)
- [ ] 10 min: Aim training / mechanical drills
- [ ] 5 min: Mental preparation (review goals, set intention)

**Focused Practice (40-60 minutes)**
- [ ] Drill specific weakness identified in plan
- [ ] Use training modes, custom games, or aim trainers
- [ ] Track performance metrics during drills
- [ ] Focus on deliberate practice, not mindless repetition

**Ranked/Competitive Play (30-40 minutes)**
- [ ] Apply drills to real match situations
- [ ] Maintain focus on 1-2 specific improvements per game
- [ ] Take notes after each game
- [ ] Stop if tilted or fatigued (max 3-4 games)

**Cooldown & Review (5-10 minutes)**
- [ ] Quick reflection on session
- [ ] Log key metrics and observations
- [ ] Identify 1 thing that went well, 1 thing to improve
- [ ] Physical cooldown (stretch, hydrate)

### 3.2 Weekly Practice Themes

**Monday: Mechanical Fundamentals**
- Focus: Aim, movement, ability execution
- Drills: Aim trainers, movement courses, combo practice
- Goal: Build muscle memory and consistency

**Tuesday: Game Sense & Positioning**
- Focus: Map awareness, positioning, timing
- Drills: VOD review, positioning exercises, decision-making scenarios
- Goal: Improve tactical awareness

**Wednesday: Champion/Character Mastery**
- Focus: Expand pool or deepen main character knowledge
- Drills: Practice new characters, study matchups
- Goal: Versatility and adaptability

**Thursday: Competitive Play & Application**
- Focus: Apply all learnings in ranked environment
- Drills: Full competitive sessions with goal tracking
- Goal: Test improvements under pressure

**Friday: Mental Game & Consistency**
- Focus: Tilt management, focus, communication
- Drills: Mindfulness exercises, VOD review of mental mistakes
- Goal: Build mental resilience

**Saturday: Team Play & Communication**
- Focus: Coordination, shotcalling, teamwork
- Drills: Duo/squad queue, focus on team plays
- Goal: Improve collaborative gameplay

**Sunday: Rest & Review**
- Focus: Recovery, analysis, planning
- Activity: Watch pro VODs, review weekly metrics, adjust plan
- Goal: Consolidate learnings and prepare for next week

### 5.2 When to Adjust the Plan

**Adjust Goals If:**
- Goals are consistently too easy (hitting targets by mid-week)
- Goals are unrealistically difficult (no progress after 2 weeks)
- Life circumstances change (less time available)
- Injury or physical issue requires reduced intensity
- Game meta shifts significantly
- Motivation is suffering due to plan structure

**Adjust Practice Methods If:**
- Not seeing improvement after 2 weeks of consistent effort
- Practice feels stale or unmotivating
- Specific drills aren't translating to match performance
- Discovering more effective training methods
- Time constraints require more efficient approach
- Burnout risk is high

**Adjust Schedule If:**
- Current schedule is unsustainable
- Missing 3+ sessions per week regularly
- Quality of practice is suffering due to fatigue
- Better time slots become available
- Need more recovery time between sessions

**How to Adjust Effectively**
1. Identify what's not working specifically
2. Research alternatives or ask for advice
3. Make one change at a time
4. Test for at least 3-5 days before further changes
5. Document what works and what doesn't
6. Don't be afraid to return to what worked before

## Appendix B: Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Practicing Without Purpose**
   - Don't just play ranked games and hope to improve
   - Always have a specific focus for each session
   - Track whether you're actually practicing what you intend to

2. **Ignoring Mental Game**
   - Tilt and burnout kill more improvement than lack of skill
   - Take breaks, manage emotions, maintain perspective
   - Gaming should be challenging but enjoyable

3. **Comparing to Others**
   - Everyone improves at different rates
   - Focus on your own progress, not others' ranks
   - Use pros as learning resources, not sources of inadequacy

4. **Neglecting Physical Health**
   - Poor posture, lack of exercise, bad sleep = worse performance
   - Take care of your body to take care of your game
   - Wrist/hand health is critical for longevity

5. **Overtraining**
   - More practice ‚â† better results if you're fatigued
   - Quality > quantity always
   - Rest days are part of the improvement process

6. **Not Reviewing VODs**
   - You can't see your mistakes in the moment
   - VOD review is where real learning happens
   - Even 20 minutes per week makes a huge difference

7. **Giving Up Too Soon**
   - Improvement is not linear
   - Plateaus are normal and temporary
   - 30 days is just the beginning of the journey
